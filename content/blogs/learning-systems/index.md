I've met many great people during my masters and later at the internships. One common thread among them, which I believed attributed to their greatness, was their ability to learn about any topic quickly. Also, more importantly, they possessed a rare talent to unlearn something fast.

### Learning Systems
From my observations the crucial aspect of learning is: it is not what you learn, but how you learn it. 

Learning System is exactly what it sounds like. A system to learning. It is an essential skill of any great scientist, engineer, doctor or an entrepreneur.

Learning System consists of but not necessarily limited to the following traits:

- Curiosity: without the _intrigue of the unknown_, the quest of knowledge cannot begin.
- An open mind: be willing to accept different assumptions and possibilities.
- Dissecting problems: Boil down a big problem to the smaller set of problems and those problems to the smallest version of it. (Recursive)
- Analogous thinking: same tool can solve multiple problems. Finding pattern is a key here. 
- Hard reset: ability to completely forgo the progress if meet the dead end and begin anew.  (Unlearning)
- Self-assessment: track and report on current progress and previous approaches.

### Conclusion
Everyone has a personal learning system. And it is crucial to improve it relentlessly in order to become the best in our respective fields. And because it is human, to learn.
